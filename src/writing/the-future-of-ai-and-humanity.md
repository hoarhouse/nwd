It is clear that the intelligence revolution has surpassed the industrial revolution in a significantly condensed timeframe. However, it appears that we are moving toward a technological age akin to the feudalistic economic order of the Middle Ages.

In that era, rulers owned the land, while those who worked the land — producing all societal value — were granted tenement rights, meaning they could work the land but had no ownership. The same dynamic is unfolding today with data. Large technology platforms possess our data, while we, the producers of this invaluable asset, are merely granted tenement status: allowed to use these platforms but owning nothing.

If data is the new oil, then we are not the oil barons but the oil.

## The Rise of Sovereign AI

When we discuss data sovereignty, we are not merely considering the control of information but the fundamental architecture of power in the digital age. Data sovereignty demands that individuals, communities, and nations retain control over their digital existence — not as tenants on platforms owned by others, but as sovereign entities with genuine agency.

Sovereign AI represents the next evolution of this principle. It is not enough to control our data if the intelligence derived from that data remains centralized in the hands of a few corporations. Sovereign AI distributes both the data and the intelligence, creating systems where AI capabilities are owned, governed, and directed by the communities they serve.

Consider the implications: instead of a handful of companies controlling AI models that shape billions of lives, we could have thousands of locally-governed AI systems, each reflecting the values, needs, and priorities of specific communities. A hospital could have its own sovereign AI trained on its data, optimized for its patients, and governed by its ethics board. A school district could develop AI tutors aligned with its educational philosophy. A nation could maintain AI systems that embody its legal framework and cultural values.

## The Antidote to Digital Feudalism

The concentration of AI capabilities mirrors and amplifies the concentration of wealth and power. When a single company controls an AI system used by billions, it effectively controls a significant portion of human decision-making. From what we see in our feeds to whom we match with romantically, from our credit scores to our job prospects — increasingly, algorithms make decisions that shape human lives.

Sovereign AI is the antidote to this digital feudalism. By distributing AI capabilities, we distribute power. By localizing control, we enable genuine democratic governance of these systems. By diversifying AI development, we prevent single points of failure that could cascade across entire societies.

The Catholic principle of subsidiarity — that decisions should be made at the most local level capable of addressing them — finds perfect expression in sovereign AI. Not every decision needs a global AI system. Not every community needs the same algorithmic priorities. Not every culture values the same outcomes.

## The Path Forward

Building sovereign AI is not merely a technical challenge but a civilizational imperative. It requires new architectures that enable distributed training and inference. It demands governance models that balance local control with beneficial coordination. It necessitates economic structures that fairly compensate data producers while enabling sustainable AI development.

This is not a call to destroy existing systems but to build alternatives. Not to wage war against technology companies but to create technologies that serve humanity. Not to retreat from the digital age but to ensure that we shape it according to our highest values.

The conversation about AI safety often focuses on preventing catastrophic risks. While important, this focus can obscure an equally vital question: even if AI remains "safe" in the narrow sense, who will it serve? Sovereign AI ensures that as artificial intelligence grows more powerful, that power flows to humanity rather than away from it.

At the Domus Communis Foundation, we are not merely advocating for these principles — we are building the technical, legal, and social infrastructures to make them reality. The future of AI and humanity is not predetermined. It will be shaped by the choices we make today about who controls data, who governs algorithms, and who benefits from artificial intelligence.

The intelligence revolution need not repeat the mistakes of history. We can build a future where AI amplifies human dignity rather than diminishing it, where communities retain sovereignty over their digital lives, and where the benefits of artificial intelligence flow to all of humanity.

That future begins with sovereign AI.
