On 24 May, the Human Communities Platform entered its international phase with a gathering in Rome convened by the Domus Communis Foundation. Cardinal Silvano Maria Tomasi, physician and Nobel co-laureate James Muller, and voices from Italy, Hungary, the United States, Ghana, and Peru came together to ask how artificial intelligence can remain in service of human dignity rather than the reverse.

The conversation moved from a single parish to a global exchange — a sign of how communities themselves are becoming active participants in shaping the future of technology. [Read the full recap](/events/may-24-guarding-data-guiding-intelligence/).
